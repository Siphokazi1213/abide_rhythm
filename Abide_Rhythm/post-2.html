The Power of Selah

In the Psalms, we often see the word Selah. Most scholars believe it means to pause and calmly think about what was just said. It is a musical direction, a breath between the notes.

"He says, 'Be still, and know that I am God...'" — Psalm 46:10

Sometimes the most faithful thing you can do is pause. In the middle of a trial or a transition, our instinct is to run faster. But faith often requires us to stand still and let God be God. A Selah moment isn't wasted time; it's worship. It's an act of faith that says, "I don't have to keep the world spinning; God has it."
